What data do we collect?

  • Data that you share with us about your skills, qualification, experience and aspirations.
  • Personal identification information that you share with us (Name, email address, phone number, etc.)
  • Information about your need for international protection. This allows us to check your eligibility for inclusion on the Global Refugee Network.
  • History of any visa applications you have made - including any potential security or health concerns. This allows us to check your potential eligibility for future visas.

How do we collect your data?

We collect the data that you share when you register on the Global Refugee Network. You can also update your data at any time by logging in to your account on the Global Refugee Network.

How will we use your data?

We share your skills, qualifications, experience and aspirations data so that we can show it to Global Refugee Network service providers. Service providers are organizations that provide services that could help you. For example education and skills training, career planning, employment services, settlement services, migration advice, etc.

We keep other data private and will only disclose it to others with your permission. In particular, we keep your name and contact details private.

How do we store your data?

We store your data securely on Amazon Web Service's cloud storage.

When will we communicate with you?

We will communicate with you regarding potential opportunities offered by organizations wanting to assist refugees and other displaced people.

We may send you reminders to complete your registration if incomplete, or to complete tasks that are overdue (for example, uploading documents that are needed for a visa application).

We may also ask you to participate in surveys or interviews for the purpose of academic research or program monitoring and evaluation. Responding to such requests is entirely voluntary and will not affect the progress of other opportunities.

What are your data protection rights?

We would like to make sure you are fully aware of all of your data protection rights. Every user is entitled to the following:

The right to access
You can access your personal data any time by logging in to your account on the Global Refugee Network.
The right to rectification
You can update your personal data any time by logging in to your account on the Global Refugee Network.
The right to erasure
You can delete your personal data any time by logging in to your account on the Global Refugee Network and requesting that your personal data is deleted.
The right to restrict processing
You are always in control of which opportunities you choose to pursue.
The right to object to processing
You can withdraw from any opportunity at any time.
The right to data portability
You are always in control of your data. Through the Global Refugee Network you can view your data, make a copy of your data, or approve the sharing of your data with others.

In summary, you are in direct control of your data at any time through the Global Refugee Network.

Cookies

Cookies are text files placed on your computer when you browse a website.

For further information, see the Wikipedia article on cookies.

How do we use cookies?

The Global Refugee Network uses cookies to improve your experience on our website, including:

  • Keeping you signed in
  • Remembering your preferences (for example, preferred language)

This is the common minimal use of cookies that is essential for the basic functioning of any website.

What type of cookies do we use?

There are a number of different types of cookies, however, our website only uses "functional" cookies which are essential for the basic functioning of any website. Functional cookies help us recognize you on our website and remember your previously selected preferences.

How to manage your cookies

You can set your browser not to accept cookies and your browser will also allow you to remove cookies.

However, the Global Refugee Network relies on its minimal use cookies for its normal operation so you should accept cookies. That will be the normal default setting of your browser. If you remove cookies you will be logged out of the Global Refugee Network (and any other websites you may be logged into). You may also lose any preferences that you have set.

Privacy policies of other websites

The Global Refugee Network may contain links to other websites. Our privacy policy applies only to the Global Refugee Network, so if you click on a link to another website, you should read their privacy policy.

Privacy policies of Global Refugee Network service providers

All Global Refugee Network service providers are required to act according to the same privacy standards as described in this policy.

Changes to this privacy policy

You can view this policy at any time by logging in to your Global Refugee Network account and selecting Privacy Policy from your account menu.

Occasionally the privacy policy may change. In that case you will be automatically asked to consent to the latest privacy policy.

How to contact a Global Refugee Network service provider

The easiest way to contact a Global Refugee Network service provider is by logging in to your Global Refugee Network account where you can message them. If a service provider has supplied an email address you will see it when you log in, and you can also email them at that address if you prefer.

How to contact another authority

Should you wish to report a complaint or if you feel that one of our service providers has not addressed your concerns in a satisfactory manner, you should contact your local office of UNHCR, the UN Refugee Agency.
